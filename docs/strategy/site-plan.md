# Miller's Marketing Group Website Discovery and Build Plan

**Prepared:** June 4, 2026<br>
**Live site reviewed:** [millersmarketinggroup.com](https://www.millersmarketinggroup.com/)<br>
**Related event source reviewed:** [Miller's Marketing Group on Eventbrite](https://www.eventbrite.com/o/millers-marketing-group-68684991773)

## 1. Executive summary

The current website presents Miller's Marketing Group (MMG) as a marketing and event-networking company serving personal-injury attorneys, doctors, real-estate professionals, and small businesses. Its strongest concrete offering is sponsorship and networking access through mixers, private luncheons, happy hours, and other events. However, the site does not yet explain the complete engagement process, clearly distinguish marketing services from event sponsorships, or provide enough proof for a prospective client or sponsor to make a confident decision.

The recommended rebuild should preserve all useful current content while reorganizing it around three clear visitor journeys:

1. **Businesses seeking marketing help:** understand what MMG does, who it serves, how engagement works, and how to book a consultation.
2. **Professionals seeking events and connections:** quickly find an upcoming event, understand who should attend, and register.
3. **Sponsors and partners:** understand sponsorship benefits, review proof from past events, and request a sponsorship conversation.

The minimum trustworthy launch should include 13 core pages/templates, with individual event detail pages generated from one structured event collection. A later growth phase can add industry landing pages, case studies, and educational resources.

## 5. Pages necessary to duplicate the current site

The following page set reproduces all meaningful current-site content without preserving the current homepage's repetition and ambiguity.

1. **Home** — overview, audiences, high-level service categories, proof, featured upcoming event, selected partners, and calls to action.
2. **About** — Andrew Miller's story, company mission, values, service area, and why MMG exists.
3. **Services** — a clear explanation of marketing strategy, relationship/network development, event marketing, and any other verified services.
4. **Sponsorships** — the current Platinum, Gold, and Silver packages, comparison table, deliverables, audience/reach data, and sponsor inquiry CTA.
5. **Partners** — verified partner/client logos with descriptions of the relationship and permission to display each logo.
6. **Events hub** — overview with Upcoming Events and Past Events views.
7. **Individual event detail template** — event name, date/time, venue, audience, agenda, hosts/sponsors, accessibility, registration, and status.
8. **Contact** — inquiry form, phone, email, address, map, scheduling, and response expectations.
9. **Privacy Policy** — required legal page.
10. **Terms of Service** — required legal page.
11. **Disclaimer** — required legal page, especially important if discussing legal/medical industries or referrals.

The Home, About, Services, Sponsorships, Partners, Events, Event Detail, and Contact pages preserve and improve every substantive area on the current site. The three legal pages preserve the current footer's stated destinations.

## 6. New pages necessary for a trustworthy website

### Trust-critical launch pages

These are not clearly present as complete pages today, but should be part of the initial trustworthy release.

| Page | Why it matters | Minimum content |
|---|---|---|
| **How It Works** | Explains what happens after a visitor contacts MMG and reduces uncertainty about the business model. | Discovery, recommended plan, execution/event participation, communication cadence, reporting, and next steps. |
| **Results / Case Studies** | Converts broad claims into evidence. | At least 2–3 approved stories with challenge, MMG work, measurable result, timeline, and client quote. |
| **FAQ** | Answers objections and clarifies the relationship between marketing, networking, sponsorships, and referrals. | Who MMG serves, geography, pricing approach, sponsor deliverables, event attendance, cancellations, and expected outcomes. |
| **Accessibility Statement** | Signals inclusion and provides a path for users who need assistance. | Accessibility commitment, known limitations, and contact method for support. |

### Recommended growth pages

These should follow once MMG can support each page with specific, non-duplicated content and proof.

- **Personal Injury Attorneys** — industry-specific outcomes, event/network value, relevant services, and proof.
- **Medical Providers / Doctors** — industry-specific positioning, compliant language, and proof.
- **Real Estate Professionals** — only if this remains an active, supported market.
- **Small Businesses** — only if MMG can define a meaningful offer beyond general marketing.
- **Insights / Resources** — useful articles, event recaps, sponsor guidance, and marketing education.
- **Team** — if MMG has team members beyond Andrew who can be publicly listed.
- **Media / Press** — if there is sufficient third-party coverage, interviews, or approved video content.
- **Thank-you pages** for contact, sponsor inquiry, newsletter signup, and event-interest conversions to improve analytics and follow-up.

### Pages not recommended until proof exists

Do not create thin pages simply to make the site look larger. Avoid publishing unsupported “industries,” “results,” or “team” pages. Trust improves when every claim is specific, verifiable, and current.

## 8. Content plan: clearly explain what MMG does

### Recommended plain-language positioning

> Miller's Marketing Group helps personal-injury professionals and other Florida businesses build valuable relationships, increase visibility, and create growth opportunities through strategic marketing, curated networking events, and sponsorship programs.

This is a working draft and should be validated with MMG leadership. The final wording must make clear whether MMG provides marketing consulting, lead generation, introductions/referrals, event production, sponsorship activation, or a combination of these services.

### Service architecture to validate

The current site mixes these ideas together. Stakeholder interviews should confirm which are real, repeatable, and commercially available:

1. **Marketing strategy and visibility**
   - Audience and market strategy
   - Campaign planning
   - Brand visibility opportunities
   - Promotional/event marketing
   - Reporting and measurement

2. **Professional networking and relationship development**
   - Curated introductions
   - Personal-injury ecosystem networking
   - Private luncheons and happy hours
   - Regional relationship-building opportunities

3. **Events and sponsorships**
   - Recurring mixers
   - Event production and promotion
   - Sponsor packages and activations
   - Speaking, booth, and brand-placement opportunities

### Content required before writing final copy

- A precise list of services MMG sells today.
- Ideal client profiles and geographic service area.
- The primary problem each service solves.
- Deliverables, timelines, and communication process.
- Pricing model or at least how proposals are scoped.
- Differentiators that can be demonstrated rather than asserted.
- Verified client count, years in business, event count, attendee count, and geographic reach.
- Approved testimonials, partner logos, and case-study data.
- Clear disclosure of what “connect you with quality attorneys and doctors” means and any compliance requirements that apply.

## 9. Upcoming events plan

Upcoming events should be easy to scan and should answer the practical questions a visitor has before clicking Register.

### Events hub behavior

- Default to the **Upcoming** view when future events exist.
- Show a clear empty state when no future event is scheduled: invite users to join the event list, follow Eventbrite, or contact MMG.
- Automatically move an event to Past Events after its end time.
- Feature the next event on the homepage.
- Let users filter by city/region, event type, and audience only if enough events exist to make filters useful.

### Upcoming event card requirements

- Event title
- Date and start/end time, including time zone
- City and venue name
- Event type
- One-sentence description
- Intended audience
- Price or “Free”
- Availability/status: registration open, waitlist, sold out, or canceled
- Registration CTA
- Sponsor CTA when sponsorships are still available
- Relevant event image with descriptive alt text

### Individual upcoming event page requirements

- Clear event summary and value proposition
- Full date, time, venue, address, map, and parking/access notes
- Who should attend and who should not
- Agenda or expected format
- Hosts, speakers, and verified sponsors
- Ticket details, refund/cancellation policy, and Eventbrite registration button
- Sponsorship availability and inquiry CTA
- Accessibility and accommodation contact
- Contact details for event questions
- Structured event data for search engines
- Calendar download/add-to-calendar action
- Related past-event recap to show what attendees can expect

### Event management recommendation

Use the website as the authoritative presentation layer while Eventbrite remains the transaction/registration platform. Store each event once in a structured content model and include the Eventbrite URL as a field. This prevents the current pattern of unlabeled image links and allows the website to create Upcoming and Past views automatically.

## 10. Past events plan

Past Events should do more than archive flyers. It should prove that MMG consistently brings the right people together and creates value for attendees and sponsors.

### Past event card requirements

- Event title, date, city, and event type
- Strong cover image
- Short outcome-focused recap
- Attendance figure, if verified
- Sponsor names/logos, with permission
- Link to full recap

### Individual past event recap requirements

- What the event was and who attended
- Key highlights or agenda
- Verified attendance and reach metrics
- Photo gallery with captions and alt text
- Short approved video highlights, not an unlabeled wall of play buttons
- Sponsor recognition and links
- Attendee/sponsor testimonials
- Measurable outcomes when available
- CTA to view the next event
- CTA to become a sponsor

### Migration approach

1. Export all events from Eventbrite.
2. Deduplicate the current homepage event links and images against that export.
3. Select the strongest 8–12 past events for rich recap pages at launch.
4. Import the remaining historical events as concise archive entries if accurate data exists.
5. Do not publish event pages that lack a title, date, location, or usable image.

## 11. Trust, quality, and compliance requirements

### Credibility and proof

- Verify every numerical claim before publication.
- Replace generic claims with specific evidence and outcomes.
- Obtain written permission for partner/client logos, testimonials, photos, and videos.
- Label relationships accurately: client, sponsor, partner, vendor, or attendee.
- Show recent activity dates so visitors know the company and event program are active.
- Explain the team and who a prospect will work with.
- Use a business-domain email and consistent phone/address information everywhere.

### Legal and industry-sensitive language

Because MMG works around legal and medical professionals, all claims about referrals, lead generation, outcomes, and professional quality should be reviewed for applicable legal, ethical, advertising, and privacy requirements. The website should not imply guaranteed outcomes or professional endorsements that cannot be substantiated.

### Forms and privacy

- Collect only the information needed for the inquiry.
- Add consent language and link to the Privacy Policy.
- Protect forms with spam prevention.
- Route general, sponsor, and event questions to the right follow-up workflow.
- Define data retention and newsletter opt-out practices.

### User experience and accessibility

- Meet WCAG 2.2 AA as the implementation target.
- Use descriptive headings, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, sufficient color contrast, and useful alt text.
- Caption videos and avoid autoplay.
- Ensure forms have labels, clear errors, and confirmation states.
- Keep mobile event registration and contact actions easy to use.

### Technical quality

- Responsive, performant pages with optimized images and videos.
- Unique titles and meta descriptions.
- Organization, LocalBusiness/ProfessionalService, Event, FAQ, and Breadcrumb structured data where appropriate.
- XML sitemap, robots controls, canonical URLs, redirects, and custom 404 page.
- Analytics for consultation clicks, phone/email clicks, sponsor inquiries, event-detail views, and Eventbrite registration clicks.
- Cookie/consent controls if required by the selected analytics and advertising tools.

## 12. Implementation roadmap

### Phase 0 — Discovery and evidence collection

**Goal:** resolve ambiguity before design and copywriting.

- Confirm MMG's exact services, business model, audiences, and geographic focus.
- Export Eventbrite data and gather the current event image/video library.
- Inventory and secure permissions for logos, testimonials, photos, and videos.
- Verify business facts, statistics, address, phone numbers, and social profiles.
- Decide whether Calendly and Eventbrite remain the booking/registration systems.
- Obtain approved legal copy or arrange legal review.

**Exit criteria:** signed-off sitemap, service definitions, proof inventory, and event migration spreadsheet.

### Phase 1 — Trustworthy minimum viable launch

**Goal:** publish a clear, credible site with a strong event experience.

Build:

- Home
- About
- Services
- How It Works
- Sponsorships
- Events hub
- Upcoming and Past Events views
- Event detail/recap template
- Partners
- Results
- FAQ
- Contact
- Legal and accessibility pages
- Redirects, SEO foundations, analytics, and form workflows

**Exit criteria:** all claims verified, all forms tested, launch events accurate, legal pages approved, accessibility review completed, and conversion tracking working.

### Phase 2 — Event archive and proof expansion

**Goal:** turn historical activity into persuasive proof.

- Publish 8–12 strong past-event recaps.
- Curate videos into named, captioned, purposeful clips.
- Add approved attendee and sponsor testimonials.
- Add verified attendance/reach metrics.
- Improve partner profiles and case studies.

### Phase 3 — Search and content growth

**Goal:** attract qualified prospects beyond direct referrals and social/event links.

- Add only the industry pages supported by distinct offers and proof.
- Publish practical event recaps and sponsor/marketing resources.
- Build local/regional event discovery pages only where inventory supports them.
- Review analytics quarterly and improve weak conversion paths.

## 14. Stakeholder questions to answer before design

1. In one sentence, what does MMG sell today?
2. Is the primary revenue source marketing retainers, sponsorship packages, event production, introductions, or another model?
3. Which audience is the highest priority: attorneys, medical providers, sponsors/vendors, real estate, or general small businesses?
4. What does MMG do after making an introduction, and how is success measured?
5. Which services can be purchased independently?
6. What geographic area does MMG actively serve?
7. Which current claims and figures can be documented?
8. Which partner logos represent clients, sponsors, vendors, or informal relationships?
9. Which past events produced the strongest attendance, sponsor, or relationship outcomes?
10. Who owns event updates, and how quickly can they publish changes or cancellations?
11. Should visitors register only through Eventbrite, or should the website collect event interest as well?
12. What are the cancellation, refund, photo/video consent, and accessibility policies for events?
13. Are there legal or professional rules governing MMG's introductions, marketing, or referral language?
14. What should happen within one business day after each type of inquiry?
