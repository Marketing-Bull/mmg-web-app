# Miller's Marketing Group Brand Voice and Website Content

**Prepared:** June 4, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define Andrew Miller's brand, establish the voice for the new website, and provide the sections and draft content needed for launch.

## 1. What the Research Reveals

### The Current Website

The current website says MMG is a marketing company, but its most convincing proof is not traditional marketing work. The strongest story is that Andrew brings personal injury professionals together through mixers, lunch and learns, private events, and sponsorships.

The current website has useful ingredients:

- Andrew's personal story and Jamaican background
- A large network of clients, sponsors, and professional relationships
- A long history of recurring events across Florida
- Marketing retainers and sponsorship packages
- Strong calls to schedule a conversation

The current website does not yet explain:

- What a marketing retainer includes
- How MMG helps someone after the first conversation
- The difference between marketing, sponsorship, and event attendance
- Why Andrew is uniquely effective at connecting people
- What sponsors receive and what success looks like
- How MMG handles professional connections without promising referrals, leads, or cases

### Andrew's Instagram

Andrew's Instagram is much closer to the real MMG brand than the current website.

Repeated themes in the posts include:

- Welcoming personal injury professionals into a relaxed environment
- Thanking sponsors, supporters, and attendees
- Building meaningful and organic connections
- Strengthening the professional community
- Making room for collaboration, learning, laughter, and fun
- Inviting people back to the next event
- Speaking warmly to "Kings and Queens," "Ladies and Gentlemen," and "my Personal Injury Professionals"
- Beginning posts with "Good Morning," "Good Afternoon," or "Grand Rising"
- Expressing gratitude, optimism, faith, and belief in community

The Instagram profile phrase **"Enough for Everyone"** is especially important. It captures Andrew's underlying belief that relationships and opportunity do not need to be treated as a zero-sum game.

### The Real MMG Brand

Andrew is not best positioned as a conventional marketing-agency executive.

He is a **trusted connector, gracious host, and community builder** who uses marketing, events, and sponsorships to help people become visible, meet the right professionals, and build relationships over time.

The new website should feel like Andrew:

- Warm, not corporate
- Confident, not loud
- Professional, not stiff
- Smooth and calm, not aggressive
- Optimistic, not inflated
- Relationship-first, not transaction-first
- Caribbean-influenced through warmth, color, hospitality, and rhythm, not through visual stereotypes

## 3. Brand Voice

### Voice Principles

#### Warm Welcome

Speak as if Andrew is welcoming someone into the room personally.

**Use:**  
"Join attorneys, medical providers, and industry partners for an evening built around meaningful conversation."

**Avoid:**  
"Leverage our premier networking platform to maximize synergistic opportunities."

#### Calm Confidence

Use simple, direct language. Do not oversell or make the page feel rushed.

**Use:**  
"We create opportunities to meet. Strong relationships grow from there."

**Avoid:**  
"Transform your business overnight with our revolutionary network."

#### People Before Transactions

Show respect for the community, sponsors, and attendees. Gratitude is a real part of Andrew's voice.

**Use:**  
"Our events are possible because of the professionals and sponsors who continue to show up for this community."

**Avoid:**  
"Gain exclusive access to high-value targets."

#### Specific and Honest

Explain what MMG does in practical terms. Do not rely on generic claims such as "innovative solutions" or "exceptional results."

**Use:**  
"MMG hosts monthly mixers, lunch and learns, and private events for Florida's personal injury community."

**Avoid:**  
"We are the leading full-service growth agency."

#### Professional With Personality

Andrew's phrases such as "Good Morning," "Grand Rising," "Kings and Queens," and "See y'all next month" are part of his social-media warmth. The website should carry the same spirit in a more polished form.

Use these phrases selectively in event updates, personal notes from Andrew, and social media. Use clearer, calmer language in core sales and compliance copy.

### Preferred Vocabulary

- relationships
- meaningful connections
- community
- collaboration
- visibility
- opportunity
- together
- welcome
- grateful
- trusted
- relaxed
- curated
- South Florida
- personal injury professionals
- show up
- follow through

### Words and Claims to Avoid

- guaranteed referrals
- guaranteed cases
- guaranteed growth
- best or number one
- approved or endorsed by The Florida Bar
- high-value targets
- exclusive access, unless it is factually true for a specific event
- generic agency language such as "disruptive," "revolutionary," and "cutting-edge"

### Example Brand Voice

> Business grows through people. MMG brings attorneys, medical providers, and trusted industry partners into the same room so they can have real conversations, build stronger relationships, and discover new opportunities together.

## 4. Visual Brand Direction

### Overall Feeling

The design should feel like a smooth South Florida evening hosted by someone who knows everyone in the room: warm, polished, relaxed, and full of possibility.

### Recommended Palette

Andrew clearly identifies with red and yellow through the MMG logo. Those colors should remain, but the website should soften and deepen them so the experience feels calm rather than loud.

| Role | Suggested color | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Deep sunset red | `#A52E2A` | Brand anchors, section accents, strong calls to action |
| Warm coral | `#D5523F` | Hover states, event accents, smaller highlights |
| Sun gold | `#F2C94C` | Logo support, badges, highlights, optimistic details |
| Mango gold | `#D99A28` | Premium sponsorship accents |
| Warm cream | `#FFF7E8` | Main page background |
| Sand | `#E8D7BC` | Cards, dividers, calm secondary surfaces |
| Deep espresso | `#231B18` | Main text and dark sections |
| Caribbean teal | `#2E7772` | Small balancing accent for calm and regional warmth |

### Color Usage

- Use cream and sand for most backgrounds.
- Use deep red for important anchors, not every surface.
- Use sun gold as a highlight, not as body text.
- Use espresso for calm contrast instead of pure black.
- Use teal sparingly to cool the red and yellow palette.

### Photography

Prioritize real photography over generic marketing imagery:

- Andrew welcoming or speaking with guests
- Candid introductions and conversations
- Small groups laughing and talking
- Sponsors engaging with attendees
- Lunch and learn speakers and audiences
- Event details that create atmosphere
- A few wide shots that show the room

Avoid building the site primarily from event flyers. Flyers should communicate event logistics, while photography should communicate the brand.

### Motion and Layout

- Smooth, slow transitions
- Generous whitespace
- Soft rounded cards and flowing section boundaries
- Subtle movement inspired by warm air, water, and conversation
- No flashing effects, aggressive counters, or crowded carousels

### Andrew's Story

Andrew Miller's entrepreneurial journey began in Kingston, Jamaica. From an early age, he learned how resourcefulness, trust, and community could create opportunity. Those lessons became the foundation for Miller's Marketing Group.

Today, Andrew brings personal injury attorneys, medical providers, and trusted industry partners together across Florida. He is known for making people feel welcome, recognizing opportunities for connection, and following through long after the first introduction.

MMG reflects Andrew's belief that business grows through relationships. The company uses marketing partnerships, sponsorships, and curated events to help professionals become visible, meet one another, and build trust over time.

### Values

**Relationships First**  
People remember how they were welcomed, treated, and supported.

**Enough for Everyone**  
Opportunity grows when the community becomes stronger.

**Show Up Consistently**  
Trust is built through steady presence and follow-through.

**Lead With Gratitude**  
Every event and opportunity is made possible by the people who participate.

**Keep It Real**  
The best professional relationships begin with honest conversation.

### Compliance and Trust

Before publishing any Florida Bar registration language, MMG should verify its current status and have the final wording reviewed.

Recommended wording if verified:

> Miller's Marketing Group is registered with The Florida Bar as a qualifying provider under Rule 4-7.22. Registration does not constitute endorsement or approval by The Florida Bar. MMG is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

Do not use the phrase "Florida Bar Qualified Referral Service" without legal review. The Florida Bar currently uses the term **qualifying provider**, and its rules prohibit implying Bar endorsement or approval.
